Here I'll show a visualization of police misconduct data from Washinton, DC.

This is from a project I've previously done for my Information Visualization class this semester. Note that this data is from 538 and is slightly outdated as the data was last updated 2/22/2021.
The following visualizations show lawsuits against the police in Washington, DC and the amount of money awarded by the city to the plaintiffs.
The left visualization shows the type of allegation against the police and the counts of each allegation. In the right visualization, each dot represents a lawsuit against the city with the x axis representing the year the lawsuit was filed and the y axis showing the amount of money awarded to the plaintiff. The size of the dot corresponds to the money awarded and the color of the dot shows the type of allegation. You can use the dropdown menu to filter towards just a certain type of allegation and you can also hover over any of the points to view more information.
It's interesting to see how the different types of police misconduct gets paid out by the city. The most common allegation, false arrest, doesnt appear to pay much, while police negligenc appears to pay out the big bucks.
The next slide shows shows the impact that colleges are having on each states Covid performance. You can pan and zoom in on the charts as well as hover over any points for more information.. The x-axis is the total number of cases that are college related, and the y-axis is the proportion of that states total college cases to the states total cases overall. The size of the dot correlates with the population of the state and its color correlates to the proportion of college cases to state cases. Note that this data is outdated circa 10/20/2020 and I made this project last semester.
This shows that colleges in big states do not contribute much to their states respective case counts, while colleges in smaller states make much more of an impact.
